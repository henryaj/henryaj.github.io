</figure><p><a href="https://www.anthropic.com/news/claude-fable-5-mythos-5">Fable</a> was released from its government-imposed shackles a couple of days ago.<span class="sidenote-wrapper"><a href="#fn-1" class="sidenote-toggle"><sup>1</sup></a><span class="sidenote"><strong>1.</strong> For those not deeply online: Fable is Anthropic&#8217;s most powerful AI model, a safer version of Mythos, which is being used to find oodles of exploits in the software which runs the world. The US Government took a dislike to it after a series of unfortunate misunderstandings and put the kibosh on it &#8211; which led to a lot of glee, given that Anthropic have themselves said that frontier labs need more oversight, but, what, not <em>that kind</em> of oversight?  Well actually yes, not the kind of oversight where a capricious state decides to just randomly do stuff with totally opaque processes. I digress.</span></span> </p><p>If you have a Claude Max plan (and I recommend you get one!) you have a couple more days of Fable access included in your plan before it becomes usage-based only, when you&#8217;ll have to pay for your usage at the normal rate, which is punishingly expensive. </p><p>For most that will just mean not using Fable, at least not until the price comes down substantially. This probably won&#8217;t happen any time soon &#8211; Opus, the next-best model, is still very expensive to use when paying the full rate.</p><p>So make hay while the sun shines, and make some things using Fable. A few things I&#8217;ve done:</p><ul><li><p><strong>&#128269; Use the <a href="https://github.com/henryaj/improve">/improve skill</a> to do wide-ranging audits on your codebases.</strong> I forked this from another author online to get it to present its as a nice HTML document to read through, and write up the top ten most pressing improvements as text plans for you to implement.</p><ul><li><p>Bonus: get Fable to dispatch subagents to implement all of those changes in separate worktrees.</p></li><li><p>Also fun: on personal projects, use it just for UX improvements. LLMs are broadly less good at this, but <a href="https://henryaj.substack.com/p/ai-has-taste-now-too">increasingly they have taste</a> and Fable especially so. Can be good for highlighting blind spots you might have developed from staring at your vibe coded projects for too long.</p></li></ul></li><li><p><strong>&#129694;If you keep a digital journal: ask Fable to give you a psychodynamic review</strong>, producing an HTML document with its analysis. I pointed it at my Obsidian vault which has a few years of journaling.</p><ul><li><p><em>This one is sort of an infohazard</em> &#8211; journals are very personal and initially Fable took a very adversarial approach, highlighting all the inconsistencies in things I&#8217;d said at different times, pointing out my various flaws and foibles, even digging into other documents (like blogposts I&#8217;d written, documents I&#8217;d highlighted etc). So be very careful what you ask for.</p></li><li><p>Necessarily your journals are not a complete accounting of your life. You probably write in them more when things are bad than when things are good, or when life is a bit empty rather than when it&#8217;s wonderfully full. So of course they give skewed impression of you.</p></li><li><p>I wrote a <a href="https://github.com/henryaj/psychobiography">kinder version of this up as an agent skill</a>.</p></li></ul></li><li><p>&#129346; Alternative to the above: <strong><a href="https://github.com/henryaj/retrospective-skill/">get Fable to write your hagiography</a></strong>. Or at least something like a &#8220;therapist on a good day gives you a review&#8221;, which was pretty fun. I also <a href="https://github.com/henryaj/the-years-retrospective">wrote this up as an agent skill</a>.</p><ul><li><p>It turns out that if you have calendar entries dating back several years, you can ask the agent to review these year-by-year as well &#8211; and this not only gives the retrospective a bit more grounding, it also can highlight some really interesting patterns in your history.</p></li><li><p>Looking back helps you <a href="https://www.goodreads.com/quotes/463176-you-can-t-connect-the-dots-looking-forward-you-can-only">connect up the dots</a>, too. Cutely enough, Fable included events and quotes from my journal which turned out to be deeply significant or foreshadowing, even if I couldn&#8217;t have known it at the time. Gives it a real sense of life being a <em>narrative</em> rather than just a series of things that happened.</p></li></ul></li></ul><figure>
