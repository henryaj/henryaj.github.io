<p>I&#8217;m back from an <a href="https://www.interrail.eu/en">Interrailing</a> trip around Europe. I&#8217;d recommend it; you can buy a pass that doesn&#8217;t cost too much and gets you a few travel days. You activate each day as you use it and get unlimited train travel for that day.</p><p>There are other passes which give you unlimited travel for a set period of time, but I think these are poor value. In practice you&#8217;ll want to stay in a place for a few days and if you want to travel locally, buying train tickets is often quite cheap.</p><p>There are caveats - some countries are very unfriendly to Interrail pass holders, with large booking fees and limited seats available on high-speed trains. But in other countries it&#8217;s fantastic, you just activate your pass and can go from Geneva to Munich in a few hours.</p><p>But the best journey I made was from Barcelona to Lyon.</p><p><a href="https://www.seat61.com/trains-and-routes/ave-s100.htm">There&#8217;s one train a day at 8:14am</a>. If you&#8217;re using your Interrail pass, the booking fee is &#8364;10. Barcelona Sants is a bit of a shit station so allow some time to get through the security checkpoint (your luggage has to be X-rayed on all high speed lines in Spain) and buy snacks from the one caf&#233; on the other side.</p><figure>

</figure><p>The trains themselves are AVE trains run by Renfe. They&#8217;re quite grand and have a sort of old-world feel to them, with an interior that harks back to the <a href="https://theweek.com/953214/plum-spilt-milk-london-restaurant-review">plum and spilt milk</a> colours of yore.</p><figure>
  <img src="https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!M_v1!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F1ac3761d-367d-42bd-9cbf-b8d61366ca82.heic" alt="" loading="lazy">
</figure><p>You leave Barcelona and do a bit of trundling along through various tunnels; nothing too impressive. But once out of the city there&#8217;s some stunning scenery. You pass the Pyrenees and can see Mount Canigou zooming past.</p><div class="native-video-embed" data-attrs="{&quot;mediaUploadId&quot;:&quot;6fd546f3-9f7a-4e61-b2bc-b026b98ae61a&quot;,&quot;duration&quot;:null}"></div><p>The train stops once it crosses the border to France and French border police board to check everyone&#8217;s documents (odd). After Perpignan, there are beautiful flooded plains (I think lakes) which run right beside the tracks.</p><figure>
  <img src="https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!sQZC!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F37db494d-852d-4a95-add0-147a081538a0_5712x4284.jpeg" alt="" loading="lazy">
</figure><p>At this point I went to the restaurant car to have an espresso with the train crew as the landscape sailed by.</p><figure>
  <img src="https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!lhzU!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff001d6f7-a0ae-453c-af5f-e123850f5644.heic" alt="" loading="lazy">
</figure><p>You go past vineyards, and across the Rh&#244;ne. And then, once you get to Vallence TGV, you <em>hurtle </em>towards Lyon at full line speed.</p><div class="native-video-embed" data-attrs="{&quot;mediaUploadId&quot;:&quot;a826a93e-3109-4a0f-8aa9-db85134ee4d1&quot;,&quot;duration&quot;:null}"></div><p>One of the best trains I took. I really felt like a journey, rather than just going from place to place.</p><p><em>I post photos of my travels on my <a href="https://www.instagram.com/henryaj/">Instagram</a>.</em></p>
