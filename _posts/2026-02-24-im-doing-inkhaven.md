</figure><p><a href="https://www.inkhaven.blog/">Inkhaven</a> is a month-long writing retreat in Berkeley, California. It runs for a month; residents have to publish 500+ words a day or they get evicted. A bit like Big Brother but for blogging, I guess.</p><p>They ran their first batch in November and it seemed to be a resounding success. The next one is coming up in April; a friend encouraged me to apply and I got a place. After a lot of hemming and hawing (is it worth the price? do I care that much about writing? is it just going to be a stressful nightmare?) I pulled the trigger this week.</p><p>I figured I was already planning a trip to the Bay anyway, to spend some time around me-shaped weirdos and absorb the local memeplex or something. The counterfactual cost isn&#8217;t so large. So why not add some structure to it?</p><p>For <a href="https://sites.google.com/site/haukehillebrandt/">my friend Hauke</a> who was in the first batch, Inkhaven made total sense. He&#8217;s an independent researcher; he lives and dies by his written output. Building his personal brand as a public intellectual is obviously important. So being in a supportive environment where he was pushed to do that and was around other people doing the same thing, as well as a bunch of famous bloggers to help workshop his writing, was robustly good.</p><p>For me, it&#8217;s not so clear. I mean, I always have &#8220;write more&#8221; as one of my goals for any given year, with varying degrees of success. (My annual goals tend to be pretty boring and nebulous though.) I enjoy writing, people commenting on and interacting with my blog, having a body of work out there for people to explore. I&#8217;m not sure any of it is really all that insightful or will stand the test of time, though I would like it to be both. I&#8217;m not even really sure <em>what </em>I want from writing, other than a creative outlet. Perhaps that&#8217;s enough.</p><p>I&#8217;m trying not to be so literal. Maybe it&#8217;s a brilliant networking opportunity in disguise. Or a chance to meet some amazing people, and new friends. Maybe it&#8217;s <a href="https://aliceisjustplaying.substack.com/p/oneshot-by-co-regulation/">co-regulation as a mental health intervention</a> disguised as a place where you write blog posts. Maybe it&#8217;s just a fun way to see the Bay with a bit more structure than &#8216;I sofa surfed and ate tacos.&#8217; Maybe it&#8217;ll show me something I&#8217;m missing: &#8220;spending thirty days with &gt;50 people on a single campus is not an experience most people get to have after their undergraduate studies.&#8221;</p><div class="embedded-post-wrap" data-attrs="{&quot;id&quot;:184378212,&quot;url&quot;:&quot;https://vishalblog.substack.com/p/for-your-consideration-inkhaven-2&quot;,&quot;publication_id&quot;:4912470,&quot;publication_name&quot;:&quot;vishalblog&quot;,&quot;publication_logo_url&quot;:&quot;https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!RIpc!,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2F4f0933ee-f7d7-4a33-b4aa-53f543c1631f_1280x1280.png&quot;,&quot;title&quot;:&quot;For Your Consideration: Inkhaven 2&quot;,&quot;truncated_body_text&quot;:&quot;The application window for Inkhaven #2 is now open.
