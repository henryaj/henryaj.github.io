<p><em>tl;dr: </em>you can subdivide Mounjaro pens to get less than the stated dose from them. This lets you e.g. buy a 15mg pen and instead get 5mg at a time out of it (so you&#8217;d get 12 doses instead of 4). This works out to be much cheaper than buying a pen of the correct dose, and cheaper than using grey market peptides. <strong>Use <a href="https://claude.ai/public/artifacts/754977a5-3757-4120-9079-5be229a109ca">this calculator</a> to figure out how much to use.</strong></p><p><em>[This is not medical advice I am not your doctor etc etc]</em></p><div><hr></div><p><em>(See also: <a href="https://lettersfrombethlehem.substack.com/p/youre-not-sick-enough-for-this-medicine">&#8220;You&#8217;re not sick enough for this medicine.&#8221;</a>)</em></p><p>Miracle weight-loss drug Mounjaro comes in fixed-dose pens and a fixed dose escalation schedule. You can 1) choose to ignore this schedule and just stay on a low dose, and 2) subdivide the pens to make them last longer and save a lot of money.</p><figure>
  <img src="https://substackcdn.com/image/fetch/$s_!oqse!,w_1456,c_limit,f_auto,q_auto:good,fl_progressive:steep/https%3A%2F%2Fsubstack-post-media.s3.amazonaws.com%2Fpublic%2Fimages%2Ff3625477-52f9-4c64-90a0-4d408f42279f_2186x1418.png" alt="" loading="lazy">
  <figcaption>I wonder if I could get one of these for my keyring?</figcaption>
</figure><p>In the US these are often autoinjector pens; there&#8217;s no way to customise the dose as it just gives you what&#8217;s printed on the side. In other countries you get a KwikPen, which is more like an insulin injector.</p><p>You take off the lid, screw on a single-use needle, twist the dial on the end until the window shows &#8220;1&#8221;, jab yourself with the needle and slowly push down the plunger.</p><p>Technically you can &#8220;count clicks&#8221;. The dial clicks as you rotate it; a full dose is 60 clicks, so 30 clicks gives you half a dose, 15 a quarter, etc. But the pen will only deliver four doses regardless of size and then it locks you out!</p><p>There&#8217;s a trivial and safe way to get around this: use an insulin needle to draw the amount of liquid that you want, and then inject it directly. You can buy the highest-dose pen, which contains four 15mg doses (60mg total) and get e.g. 24 &#215; 2.5mg doses from it. This means you can stretch out a single pen to last many months.</p><p>The cost savings are substantial &#8211; a 2.5mg pen costs &#163;37.24 per dose, but a 15mg pen is only slightly more expensive. So if you&#8217;re using a 15mg pen but only taking 2.5mg at a time, it works out at &#163;12.46 per dose. This is less even than grey market Chinese peptides, which often run $100 for 10mg.<span class="sidenote-wrapper"><a href="#fn-1" class="sidenote-toggle"><sup>1</sup></a><span class="sidenote"><strong>1.</strong> Based on prices listed at <a href="https://onlinedoctor.asda.com/uk/mounjaro.html">ASDA Online Doctor</a> as at publication. Example per milligram prices:  2.5 mg: &#163;148.97 &#247; 10 mg = <strong>&#163;14.90/mg </strong>5 mg: &#163;188.97 &#247; 20 mg = <strong>&#163;9.45/mg </strong>7.5 mg: &#163;248.97 &#247; 30 mg = <strong>&#163;8.30/mg </strong>10 mg: &#163;278.97 &#247; 40 mg = <strong>&#163;6.97/mg </strong>12.5 mg: &#163;288.97 &#247; 50 mg = <strong>&#163;5.78/mg </strong>15 mg: &#163;298.97 &#247; 60 mg = <strong>&#163;4.98/mg  </strong>So even using a 5mg pen for 2.5mg doses is a substantial cost savings, especially added up over a long period of time &#8211; most patients will take Mounjaro for months or years to get to their goal weight, and often will stay on a 2.5mg a week maintenance dose indefinitely.</span></span></p><h2>Don&#8217;t the pens expire?</h2><p><strong>Yes, and this is where this trick runs counter to the manufacturer&#8217;s advice.</strong></p><p>Once you start using the pen, it&#8217;s been exposed to the air and to any bacteria on the needle. There <em>is </em>a risk of bacterial growth in the liquid in the pen &#8211; the typical guidance is to not use an opened multidose vial beyond 30 days &#8211; but this is probably overstated.</p><p>The tirzepatide solution in the pen contains benzoyl alcohol which inhibits bacterial growth, although not forever. In the case of e.g. multi-dose insulin vials, using them well beyond 28 days shows negligible contamination in practice &#8211; one study found only trace skin flora after 53 days of use, another found the preservatives actively killed deliberately introduced bacteria through day 50, and a third found zero contamination across six months of twice-daily use<span class="sidenote-wrapper"><a href="#fn-2" class="sidenote-toggle"><sup>2</sup></a><span class="sidenote"><strong>2.</strong> In a <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/3935593/">study of 69 multi-dose insulin vials used by patients for an average of 53 days</a>, only 8 showed any bacterial contamination at all &#8212; just 1 colony-forming unit per millilitre of common skin flora (<em>S. epidermidis</em> and <em>P. acnes</em>), with no endotoxin detected. Critically, when vials were deliberately inoculated with <em>S. aureus</em> and <em>P. aeruginosa</em> and kept at room temperature, they were sterile within 48 hours &#8212; and this antibacterial effect was maintained through serial re-contamination at days 17, 30, and 50.  A <a href="https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37559404/">more recent study went further</a>: refrigerated multi-dose insulin vials aspirated twice daily with a new syringe for six months showed no microbial contamination at any point during the study period.  A <a href="https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1111/jsap.13664">human retrospective study of insulin glargine</a> used up to 74 days beyond the recommended duration found no injection site infections.</span></span>. <a href="https://old.reddit.com/r/MounjaroAus/comments/1o09yvi/whats_the_longest_youve_used_your_pen_for/">Anecdotally people have used the pens for many months after they&#8217;re first used without issue</a>.</p><p>But the medical guideline is to use the pens for no longer than 30 days after opening. It&#8217;s up to you to decide your risk appetite here. But plenty of users don&#8217;t need to go to the higher doses, with <a href="https://old.reddit.com/r/Mounjaro/comments/1ad1bt7/has_anyone_stayed_on_25mg_the_whole_time/">many staying at 2.5mg</a>. There&#8217;s <a href="https://www.secondnature.io/us/guides/weight-loss-medications/optimal-mounjaro-dose">no good reason to increase your dose</a> if you&#8217;re seeing satisfactory results at lower doses.</p><p>You can even take 1.25mg, or half the starting dose, if you&#8217;re concerned about side-effects when you start.</p><h2>Guide to getting smaller doses</h2><p>You&#8217;ll need an insulin needle and a Mounjaro KwikPen.
